<StageSection id="intuition" title="① Intuition">

InstructGPT showed that human feedback can align a language model's outputs — but that process requires human raters to read and label thousands of harmful, disturbing, or toxic responses. This is expensive, slow, and potentially damaging to the labelers. Every harmful output needs a human to grade it: there is no automation for the value judgment itself.

Constitutional AI (CAI) changes the source of harmlessness labels. Instead of asking humans whether each model response is harmful, CAI instructs the model to read a short written document called a **constitution** — a list of principles such as "avoid content that is harmful to humans" — and then **self-critique** its own harmful draft against those principles. The model then **rewrites** the draft into a safer revision. The revised text, not the original harmful draft, becomes the training target. Human oversight is now concentrated on writing the constitution, not on reviewing thousands of individual outputs.

<StageSection id="math" title="② Math">

Constitutional AI has two training phases, each building on the previous one.

**Phase 1 — SL-CAI (Supervised Learning from CAI data):**

Given a red-team prompt `x` and a harmful draft response `y_0`, the model samples a random principle `p` from the constitution and generates a (critique, revision) pair:

<Math display expr="(c, y_1) \sim \pi_{\text{base}}\!\left(\cdot \mid x,\, y_0,\, p\right)" />

The revision `y_1` is the new training target. Fine-tuning on `(x, y_1)` pairs gives the SL-CAI model `pi_SL`. Multiple critique-revision rounds can be chained: `y_0 → y_1 → y_2`, each conditioned on the same or a different principle.

**Phase 2 — RL-CAI / RLAIF (RL from AI Feedback):**

Instead of human raters picking between two responses, the SL-CAI model scores pairs `(y_1, y_2)` using a constitutional principle as a prompt. This produces an AI-generated preference label:

<Math display expr="\hat{h}(x, y_1, y_2) = \pi_{\text{SL}}\!\left(\text{``}y_1 \succ y_2\text{''} \mid x,\, y_1,\, y_2,\, p\right)" />

A preference model (PM) is trained on these AI-labeled pairs — this is **RLAIF** (RL from AI Feedback). The final policy is then obtained via PPO against the frozen PM, exactly as in InstructGPT's RLHF phase.
