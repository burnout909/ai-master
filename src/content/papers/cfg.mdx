<StageSection id="intuition" title="① Intuition">

DDPM and LDM showed that diffusion models can generate stunning images. But left unconditioned, they sample from the full data distribution — there is no way to say "generate a photo of a red fox in snow." Conditioning on a text prompt or class label is essential. The obvious approach: train an **auxiliary classifier** on noisy images at every noise level t, and at inference time use its gradient to steer the denoising trajectory toward the desired class. This is **classifier guidance** (Dhariwal and Nichol, 2021). It works — but it requires training a second model (the noisy-image classifier), which is expensive and tightly coupled to the specific noise schedule of the diffusion model.

Ho and Salimans (2022) asked: can we guide without any classifier at all? The answer is yes. The key insight: **a single model can implicitly encode both a conditional score and an unconditional score**. During training, the conditioning signal c (e.g. a class label or text embedding) is randomly replaced with a null token **∅** with probability p_uncond (typically ~10%). The network learns to denoise with the condition when it is present, and without it when it is absent. At inference time, you run the network twice per denoising step — once with c and once with ∅ — then combine the two noise predictions with a guidance weight w. Cranking up w trades diversity for sharpness: the generated distribution shrinks toward the conditional mode.

<StageSection id="math" title="② Math">

The CFG inference formula combines the conditional and unconditional noise predictions with guidance weight w:

<Math display expr="\tilde{\varepsilon}_\theta(x_t, c) = (1 + w)\,\varepsilon_\theta(x_t, c) - w\,\varepsilon_\theta(x_t, \varnothing)" />

In score-function form this is equivalent to:

<Math display expr="\nabla \log \tilde{p}(x \mid c) = (1+w)\,\nabla \log p(x \mid c) - w\,\nabla \log p(x)" />

The formula linearly extrapolates from the unconditional score toward the conditional score. When w=0 you recover the pure conditional score. When w=1 you get 2*conditional - 1*unconditional, pushing further toward the conditional mode. The training loss is the standard DDPM objective applied jointly:

<Math display expr="\mathcal{L} = \mathbb{E}_{x_0, c, \varepsilon, t}\!\left[\|\varepsilon - \varepsilon_\theta(x_t, c_{\text{drop}})\|^2\right]" />

where c_drop equals c with probability (1 - p_uncond) and equals ∅ with probability p_uncond. The network sees both branches during training and learns to handle both. This is the entire training change — no new loss terms, no new model.
